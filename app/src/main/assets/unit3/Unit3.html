OBJECT OF ACT

Definition

Factory is any premises including

1) 10+ employee working on any day or were in 12 months,and in any part of which manufacturing process carried out with aid of power,or ordinarily carried on ,

or

2) 20+ employee working on any day or were in 12 months, and in any part of which manufacturing process carried out without aid of power.

#premise-land or land with building

#precinct-space enclosed by wall

#Manufacturing process-any process making alterations, repairing , finishing , packing ,oiling,etc substance to use        Pumping oil,gen power,constructing,storing

#Worker-person who is employed,employed by agency,employed in any manufacturing process

#Occupier-person who controls the affairs of factory        

DUTIES of Occupier

  • Provide information ,training
  • Look after employees

MRTP ACT 1969(Trade Policy Act)

Monopolies Restrictive Trade Practices

Applies to whole india

Objectives-

  • Prevent concentration of economic power
  • Control of monopoly,restrictive,unfair trade practices
  • Prevent use of economy as socio economic objective

Basic provisions-Mrtp act

        Monopoly’s vis a vis concentration of economic power

        Monopolistic restriction of unfair trade practices

1) Monopoly’s vis a vis concentration of economic power

Aims-

  • Industry with economic power identified
  • These identified industries  are made to register
  • Their merging with other company should be approved by government
  • Directors are brought under scrutiny
  • Government may force sometime to / into smaller divisions
  • Government approval , public interest (if prejudice) can disapprove

2) Monopolistic restriction of unfair trade practices

Previous view (concentration of power) was criticized due negative impact

New policy in 1991

Aims-

  • Prior approval of central government not required for merging, establishing,

             new directors.

  • Important to control unfair trade practices
  • Transfer of share should be in company’s act
  • Strengthen mrtp commissioned to take action wrt monopolistic,unfair trade practices
  • MRTP commission can encourage to investigate on its own or public

             complain.

  • Government can breakup concentration of economic power

NAVRATNA COMPANIES

Navaratna Companies :  those which score 60/100.

Score is evaluated based upon 6 factors:-

  1. profit/work
  2. production cost
  3. PBDIT(profit before depreciation interest and taxes)
  4. PBDIT to turn over
  5. earning per share
  6. inter sectoral performance

Freedom to navratna companies

  1. Capital expenditure
  2. Entering into joint venture
