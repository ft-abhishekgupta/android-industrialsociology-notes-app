L-3

Development Of Industry

THE PRIMITIVE/BARTER STAGE

Features-

  • Family self sufficient
  • no problem of exchange of goods
  • domestication of animals
  • barter economy - direct exchange of one commodity for another
  • surplus goods for a person  --> then exchange

Decline-

  • Some family had surplus while other deficient

AGRARIAN ECONOMIC STAGE

Features-

  • Agriculture primary source
  • village became unit of economy and self sufficiency
  • not at family level
  • Rise of traders
  • use of animal in cultivation
  • rise of political institution
  • irrigation and cultivation techniques

HANDICRAFT STAGE

  • For local population
  • In exchange various things from Customer
  • simple hand tools and Manual skill
  • no division of labour
  • simple industry
  • craft men responsible for everything

GUILD SYSTEM

Guild is an association of craftsmen in a particular (Trade which has exclusive right to produce certain goods and services

Features-

  • Craftsmen in same line
  • in town or village
  •  Aims to protect interests and implement rules
  • quality goods or punishment
  • Welfare of members
  • two types
  1. Merchant guild - Association of Merchant to info equality of opportunity to member
  2. Craft men guild - Association of skilled artisans in same occupation to regulate entry and conduct

Decline-

  • narrow outlook of craftsmen
  • restriction of entry of new member
  • lack of commitment
  • development of new towns without guild
  • opposition from journeymen

FEUDAL/MANORIAL SYSTEM

Socio Political structure in which a  monarchy tries to control the land of the realm through reciprocal agreement with regional leaders

Features-

  • Land belonged to king who gave their lands to feudal Lords who loan this    

             land to peasants to work .

  • slavery
  • Capitalism
  • class distinction
  • every man bound to another
  • land in return of Military Service
  • social and economic
  • Lord-owner    /     Vassal-a holder of land   /    Fief-land

Decline-

  • Rent to Lords
  • no free will
  • not free to leave land
  • mistreatment
  • overexploited
  • more tax

PUTTING OUT/DOMESTIC SYSTEM

Features-

  • Work from home
  • entrepreneur provides material, assigns work, inspects and markets the

             product

  • market at Central place
  • cloth production popular
  • bypass guild system
  • workers better treated
  • women can work
  • Work at own speed

Decline-

  • Due to industrial revolution
  • more demand
  • large scale production needed
  • less economy
  • minimum mechanization

Industrial Revolution

CHANGES

  • Development of Engineering
  • evolution in iron making
  • use of power driven machines
  • price of chemical industry
  • development of coal mining
  • development of means of transport

ECONOMIC EFFECTS

  • Large scale production
  • change of form of ownership
  • specialisation
  • rise of capitalism
  • Trade cycle
  • standard of living

SOCIAL AND POLITICAL EFFECT

  • Urbanisation
  • rise of individualism
  • awareness of rights
  • poor working condition
  • political awareness

PRE MACHINE STAGE

Features-

  • Manual labour
  • small scale
  • local market distribution by merchants
  • no competition
  • inspection of goods
  • traditions have influence
  • agriculture measure economy
  • simple form
  • lack of transport
  • metallic currency

FACTORY SYSTEM

Impact

  • Less physical labour
  • Low cost
  • More capital
  • New pattern of social satisfaction

Negative Impact

  • Fatigue boredom
  • Competition
  • Exploitation
  • Long working hours

L-4

Industrialisation

It is the process of social and economic change whereby a society is transformed from a pre industrial society to an industrial one.

CHARACTERISTICS OF INDUSTRIALISATION

  • Large scale mechanization
  • automatic
  • scientific methods
  • division of labour
  • occupation
  • better utilisation of resources
  • mass production
  • Large capital
  • inventions

CAUSES OF INDUSTRIAL REVOLUTION / FACTORY SYSTEM CHARACTERISTICS

  • Large scale production
  • mechanization
  • automation
  • specialisation
  • standardization
  • management information system
  • growth of Industrial Area
  • expansion of international trade
  • research and development
  • competition
  • demand

CONSEQUENCES OF INDUSTRIAL REVOLUTION

On Marriage

  • Marriage as Social contract
  • marriage at higher age
  • practice of non marriage
  • practice of love marriage due to no restrictions
  • increase in divorce rate due to free will

On Family

  • breaking of joint family
  • nuclear family
  • status of women degraded
  • disintegration of family
  • changes in family goals
  • role of family

On Caste System

  • Disintegration of caste system
  • reduction in influence of brahmins
  • division of labour based on specialisation
  • abolition of caste based function
  • Abolition of control of caste
  • not follow untouchability
  • inter caste marriages

On Religion

  • Decline of superstition
  • development of religious tolerance
  • secular state

On Morals

  • Rational Outlook
  • materialistic Outlook
  • individualism
  • increase crime
  • loosening of social control
  • less focus on entertainment
