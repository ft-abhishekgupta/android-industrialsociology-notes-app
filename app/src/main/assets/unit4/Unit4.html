L 7

Grievance Handling Procedure

GRIEVANCE

Grievance can be written or verbal

According to Keith - “It is real or imagined feeling of personal injustice which an employee has concerning his employment relationship”

Forms of grievance

  1. Factual - for genuine reason
  2. Imaginary - because of wrong perception
  3. Other - Actual reason not known , due to pressure / frustration

CAUSES

Working conditions

  • bad environment
  • poor communication
  • improper job and skill matching
  • change in schedules
  • no proper tools
  • tight production
  • unhealthy supervisory practices
  • Social distance
  • bad employer employee relation

Management policy

  • less wages
  • Less leaves
  • overtime
  • No promotion
  • transfer
  • lack of development of employees
  • Implementation of personnel policies
  • difference in opinion
  • Attitude of Management

Personal

  • over ambition
  • excess self esteem
  • impractical attitude

EFFECT

#SAME AS STRIKE# (Strike is discussed later , in Lesson-8)

MODEL GRIEVANCE PROCEDURE

Grievance Handling

Conciliation- resolving industrial conflict with the help of 3rd party

Court of Inquiry- government appoint it to Submit report in 6 months, 1 or more persons, right to strike, etc. unaffected

Voluntary Arbitration- resolving industrial conflict by an independent person, saves time and money

Adjudication - resolving dispute by labour court or tribunals (3rd party by government)

Guidelines

  • Complaints should be heard
  • Superior should try to get to the root of the problem
  • Management should try to remove grievance if real
  • If grievance is imaginary then employee should be convinced
  • Grievance should be put to write
  • feedback

ESSENTIALS OF GRIEVANCE PROCEDURE

  • acceptable
  • simple
  • training
  • follow up - review
  • Promptness - speed
  • conformity with existing legislation

L-10

 Industrial Relation Machinery

INDUSTRIAL DISPUTE SETTLEMENT MACHINERY

  1. Conciliation
  2. Court of enquiry
  3. Voluntary arbitration
  4. Adjudication

Conciliation

        Is a method of resolving the industrial dispute with the help of 3rd party, who intervenes in the dispute situation upon request.

  • assist in negotiation
  • removes bottleneck
  • it is a form of mediation that is to make conflicting parties to compromise
  • government appoints conciliation officer or board of conciliation

        Conciliation officer

  • Appointed by government or deputy commissioner of labour
  • Have power of civil court
  • Persuade both parties to come to settlement
  • Submit report of success or failure to gov in 14 days

        Board of conciliation

  • If conciliation officer fails
  • Tripartite body with chairman and 2-4 members
  • Submit report of success or failure to government in 2 months
  • Persuade both parties to come to settlement

Appraisal

Inefficient , low education , training , experience , parties’ casual attitude

Effectiveness can be Increased by

  • Training , selection , additional power , no political interference , independent

Court of inquiry

 If conciliation fails government can appoint it to enquire to any matter in ind dispute

  • Report in 6 months
  • Published by government in 30 days
  • Rights to lockout , strike , etc. safe
  • Aims to reveal cause of dispute , promote settlement

Appraisal

25% of cases goes to it , many rejected , lack of legal understanding

Voluntary arbitration

It refers to getting dispute settled through an independent person chosen by the parties involved mutually and voluntarily

  • Saves money time , democratic , best , fast , self governance
  • Method advocated by Mahatma Gandhi in textile industry ahmedabad
  • Parties sign written agreement - arbitration agreement
  • Government publish it under 1 month under Industrial Dispute Act
  • One or more , odd , umpire(to give arbitration award)
  • Have no judicial powers
  • When awarded , government enforces it under 1 month for the specified period or 1 year
  • Prohibit strike, lockout when award in operation , arbitration pending

Appraisal

2-3% only , not much progress

Due to legal obligation, cost, less confidence,failure

Adjudication

Include - labour court,industrial tribunals, national tribunals

labour court

  • One member - sitting / ex-judge of high court
  • Issues include
  1. Dismissal of worker
  2. Standing orders
  3. Strike lockout
  4. Withdrawal of privileges

Industrial tribunals

  • One member
  • Wider jurisdiction
  • Can have 2 advisors
  • Issues include
  1. Wages
  2. Working hours
  3. Holidays
  4. Allowance
  5. Discipline
  6. Shifts , bonus

National tribunals

  • Publishes within one month
  • Include
  1. Questions of national importance
  2. Dispute involving one or more states

Appraisal

Greater benefits , good condition , importance

Not satisfactory as delays , inefficient

Tripartite and Bipartite Machinery

CIRM Central Industrial Relation Machinery

Setup in 1945 with chief labour commissioner as the head of department

To give effect to the various provisions as the industrial dispute act

  • In delhi
  • 31 officers perform staff func
  • 18 chief labour commissioner - head of regions, 70 assistant , 160 forcement officers
  • Spread as Zonal and regional level

Functions

  1. Settlement and prevention of dispute
  2. Enforcement of laws
  3. Verification of membership of trade union
  4. Adhoc verification of membership
  5. Verification of membership of registered trade union to central trade organisation
  6. Conduct enquiry into breaches of discipline
  7. Promotion of work committees , participation in management
  8. Defence of count cases and write petitions of laws

MACHINERY

TRIPARTITE

Set up by ILO to form a organisation sufficiently large

In 4th labour conference 1942

To provide forum of discussion and consultation on various labour related issues

These include

  • Indian labour conference
  • Standing labour conference
  • Industrial committees
  • State labour advisory board

The government ensured

  1. Equality of representation i.e. Parity of representative
  2. Nomination
  3. Representation of certain interests

BIPARTITE

  • Include work committees and Joint Management Council (JMC)
  • Consultative , not negotiating body
  • Deals with disputes
  • The Industrial Truce Resolution 1947 - to set up wc
  • First 5 yr Plan - to set up JMC
  • Work committee

Work committee  (WC)

Effective social institution of industrial democracy and as a statutory body established within the industrial unit which represents management and workmen

Objectives

  • Remove causes of friction
  • Promote measures security amity
  • Established relationship
  • Strengthen spirit of voluntary settlement

Functions

WILL DEAL WITH

  • Condition of work
  • Amenities
  • Safety
  • Welfare and fine fund
  • Education and recreation
  • Festival and national holiday

EFFECTIVENESS DEPENDS OF

  • Responsive attitude, adequate support,appreciation, implementation,co-ordination

Joint management council (JMC)

Greater sense of participation , mutual interaction between labour and management ,

Work committee open door for trade union to acquire knowledge of discipline , efficiency

  • Ensure operation of private owned concern
  • Better understanding of employees

Functions

  • Welfare measures , supervision , training , work schedule , holidays
  • Right to information to discuss
  • Economic situation
  • State of market
  • Method of manufacture
  • Consult to matters
  • To improve productivity
  • Serve employees
  • Management can consult for
  • Orders and amendments needed
  • Reduction of operation
  • New method to introduce
  • Supervision of safety

Failure Of Bipartite

  1. Lack of follow up measures
  2. Lack of positive response
  3. Trade union not very strong
  4. Emphasis of participation at higher level
